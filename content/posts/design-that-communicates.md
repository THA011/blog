Ask most people what a brand is and they'll point at a logo. But a logo is just a
signature. The brand is everything that signature promises — and design is how
you keep that promise visible, everywhere, consistently.

## Consistency builds recognition

The same colours, type and spacing repeated across your website, social posts and
documents teach people to recognise you at a glance. Every time you break the
pattern to be "creative," you reset that recognition to zero. Consistency isn't
boring — it's how trust compounds.

## Contrast creates clarity

Good design guides the eye. Contrast — between big and small, bold and light,
colour and neutral — tells the viewer what matters most. Low-contrast design
looks timid and flat; considered contrast looks confident and makes the important
thing unmistakable.

## Restraint signals confidence

Amateur design tries to use every colour, font and effect at once. Professional
design removes until only what's necessary remains. Two typefaces, a small
palette, and plenty of space almost always beat a page fighting with itself.

> People decide whether they trust you before they've read a single sentence.
> Design is that first, silent argument.

## Design should sound like your words

Your visuals and your copy are two halves of one voice. A playful brand shouldn't
look austere; a serious service shouldn't look like a toy. When I design a brand,
I start from how it should *feel* to the customer — then make the type, colour and
layout say the same thing the words do.
